English Metrical Feet

Iamb (iambic)

Unstressed + Stressed (˘ —)

Example: "to-DAY", "a-RISE", "shall I COM-pare"

Trochee (trochaic)

Stressed + Unstressed (— ˘)

Example: "TI-ger", "DOU-ble", "TELL me NOT"

Anapest (anapestic)

Unstressed + Unstressed + Stressed (˘ ˘ —)

Example: "in-ter-VENE", "of a VOICE"

Dactyl (dactylic)

Stressed + Unstressed + Unstressed (— ˘ ˘)

Example: "THIS is the FOR-est", "PO-et-ry"

Spondee (spondaic)

Stressed + Stressed (— —)

Example: "HEART-BREAK", "KNOCK KNOCK"

Pyrrhic (pyrrhic)

Unstressed + Unstressed (˘ ˘)

Example: (substitution foot in iambic)

Key Prosodic Concepts

लઘુ (Laghu)

Light syllable = 1 matra. Short vowels, unstressed syllables in English.

ગુરુ (Guru)

Heavy syllable = 2 matras. Long vowels, consonant clusters, stressed syllables.

यति (Yati)

Caesura/pause position in classical meters. Regulates rhythm.

Scansion

Marking stressed (/) and unstressed (˘) syllables in a line.

Caesura

Mid-line pause. Can be masculine (end of foot) or feminine (within foot).

Enjambment

Line break without punctuation. Creates momentum vs. end-stopped lines.
